ABOUT OUR PROJECT

Katakhali Swapnopuron Welfare Society (KSWS) is an organization founded in 2012 by Ms Satarupa Majumdar, to improve lives of the children living in the remote villages in islands of Sunderbans. The foundation of KSWS provides world- class, English medium education, CBSE curriculum to 800 +children through its schools. simultaneously, empowering the community with livelihoods, awareness and relief work initiatives

OUR MISSION
To empower children with holistic,quality education. To create a safe fair community through building knowledge and livelihoods

OUR VISION
A safe empowered community where every child has an equal chance to realize his/her full potential.

Empowering Little Minds: A Mathematical Journey

In the Main Building, Campus Katakhali, Sucharita Seal, one of our eminent trainers and counsellors, empowered 20 pre-primary teachers through mathematics education kits. The teachers embraced innovative ways to teach. Colourful counting blocks, puzzles, and games transformed classrooms into magical realms of math. Laughter and exploration replaced rote learning, as children eagerly solved problems and built strong foundations after the training session. Parents are quiet marvelled at their children's newfound enthusiasm. Sucharita's vision became a reality, igniting a passion for learning within each child. Thanks to Sucharita and the dedicated teachers, and special thanks to the AluDecor team for making it possible.



Education Kit Distribution

Kit Distribution

This initiative was a small step towards creating a more equitable and inclusive society, where all children have access to quality education and learning resources. We hope that this gesture will inspire more people to come forward and make a difference in their own small ways.

The education kits included a range of materials such as english worksheet, bengali worsheet, along with some more informational equipments. The mothers were also provided with information on how to use the materials to support their children's learning at home.

The education kit distribution drive for the mothers of underprivileged children was a meaningful and impactful initiative that helped to promote the importance of education and parental involvement in our community. It was a reminder that education is a collective responsibility and that we all have a role to play in ensuring that every child has access to quality education and learning resources.
